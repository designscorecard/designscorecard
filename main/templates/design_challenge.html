2020 Design Thinking Challenge (COVID-19 Response)

This Design Challenge puts you through a thinking process on how to solve problems with any ideas, just as long as that idea can be understood and put into writing. Everyone has an interest in the development of the world around them, therefore it is of utmost importance that we come together to share those ideas, notwithstanding your educational, financial or professional background.

According to CBN, over 123,600 people have been affected globally and this has led to a decline in the economy and everyday living. The price of the most reliant Crude Oil has also been affected falling to $17 per barrel. According to International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI), about 37 countries have enacted various forms of food restrictions in response to COVID-19

The Design Challenge sets the tone for bringing in ideas from different points of view in order to get a large pool of people’s suggestions. To make things clear, the term “Design” does not necessarily mean a visual look or shape given to a certain object, to put it right, “Design Thinking”is all about providing clarity and solutions based approach to problem solving.

That is to say; this challenge is not restrictive to designers or architects alone (let the title "design Challenge" not give you that impression), it accommodates anyone and everyone can think of ways to curb most of the issues we have as humans, putting in various initiatives by which these ideas and concepts can reach the right people.

The Challenge - Food Security

This challenge brings together citizens who can think of ways to curb most of the issues we have. Specifically, we have narrowed it down to our country Nigeria and its most prevalent issues. However, seeing that we cannot address it all, we would first focus on the high rate of food insecurity and hunger, especially during these times of the COVID19 Pandemic. How to participate

1

State your Challenge.

The goal is to be as creative as possible. Brainstorm on ways by which food resources can be distributed in the most efficient way possible. (Put into consideration that we would be dealing with both the beneficiaries and distributors).

2

Generate three (3) initial concepts.

Come up with a minimum of three (3) concepts. Sketch out your concept or idea in drawing or writing; this is just a sketch so it doesn't have to be completely perfect. Ensure it is as descriptive as possible for easy referencing, share with people around you to get feedback on these.

3

Reduce the number of your concepts.

Narrow your concepts down to two (2), review these and single out the one you feel is the best for the problem addressed.

4

Fine-tune your Ideas.

If you are a designer, use wireframe (XD, Illustrator or any graphic design) tools to make your idea more visible. Other non-design tools can be used to describe the ideas (an essay, PowerPoint, pictorial representation) as long as it is well descriptive

5

Submit your ideas.

Once we review your ideas and it’s shortlisted, we will contact and add you to the community where the implementations will commence. Goodluck!Download Instructions
