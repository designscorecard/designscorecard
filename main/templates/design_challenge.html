Join the Community - The 10 Plus 10 Method

1

State your design challenge.

The goal is to be as creative as you can be. Brainstorm on possible means for food distribution (considering the fact that we will be dealing with both the receiver and the giver).

2

Generate three (3) initial concepts.

Try to sketch your concept on a paper (don't worry if your sketching does not look beautiful at first). Make sure you add descriptions to every of your sketch for easy references.

3

Reduce the number of design concepts.

Streamline and reduce the number of your concepts to two (2). Review your concept and remove the one with lesser merit.

4

Present your best idea(s) to a group.

You don't have to do this alone: there are ways to preserve these captured ideas so you can share and discuss them with your colleagues and friends. Gather as many feedback as possible from them.

5

Change your sketches.

As you keep getting new ideas, update your sketches.

6

Fine-tune your Ideas.

If you are a designer, use wireframe (XD, Illustrator or any graphic design) tools to make your idea more visible. And for those who can't use the tools, make sure your descriptions and sketches are easy to read and understand.
